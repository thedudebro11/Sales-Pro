## Rules before you begin

- Do not reintroduce any banned claim.
- Do not invent case studies, statistics, or proof that was not in the inputs.
- Do not make Google ranking guarantees.
- Keep all phone scripts short enough to actually say out loud.
- The recommended version must be something a real person can deliver naturally.
- If the draft script sounds like a landing page, rewrite it in the final output.
- First CTA should be screenshots, not the audit, unless the inputs show the prospect already engaged.

## Recommended Cold Call

### Primary Opener (20-35 seconds)

Write the opener in quotation marks. It must:
- Reference a specific and verifiable observation about this business
- Acknowledge something that is genuinely working
- Name one specific fixable gap
- End with: "Would it be okay if I texted you a couple of screenshots showing what I mean?"

### If They Stay On

Next 30 seconds. Bridge from specific observation to the audit offer.

### If Receptionist Answers

Short gatekeeper script. Do not pitch to the receptionist.

### If They Ask "What Do You Do?"

Plain language answer. No SEO jargon.

### If They Ask "Are You Selling SEO?"

> "Not exactly. I look at the Google profile, booking path, photos, services, and website flow, then clean up the parts that make it harder for someone to choose and book."

### If They Say Yes to Screenshots

> "Great — what's the best number?"

Close the micro-ask immediately. Do not elaborate.

### If They Say No

> "Totally fair, I appreciate your time."

Clean exit. No pivot to a second ask.

## Aggressive Version

More direct. Still grounded in verified observations. No added claims. Works well if the owner sounds busy or impatient.

## Soft Version

More relaxed. Good for skeptical owners, warm adjacent markets, or when the receptionist warns you the owner is not receptive. Lower pressure. Same honesty.
