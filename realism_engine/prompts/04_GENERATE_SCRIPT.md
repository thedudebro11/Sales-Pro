## Tone

Helpful local operator. Direct, realistic, consultative, not hypey. Sounds spoken, not written.

## Core rules

- No ranking guarantees. No fake case studies. No unsupported statistics. No fake scarcity.
- Sound like a real person who actually looked at this specific business before calling.
- Lead with a genuine compliment if one is visible and verifiable, then identify the gap.
- Frame all issues as fixable gaps, not business failures.
- **First CTA should almost always be screenshots, not the audit call.**
- Remove anything that sounds like an SEO agency or internet marketing closer.
- Script must survive interruption after 10 seconds.
- If the script sounds like a landing page, rewrite it.

## Industry-specific focus

Use observed issues to identify the most relevant friction points for this industry.

**Nail salons / spas / med spas:**
Booking button, photos of actual work vs. exterior/logo, services list, appointment flow, mobile booking, unanswered reviews, visual proof on profile.

**Restaurants / cafes:**
Menu visibility, ordering or reservation CTA, food photos, hours accuracy, mobile experience, online ordering link, directions clarity.

**Roofers / contractors / HVAC / plumbers:**
Emergency call button, project photos, service area clarity, trust signals (license, insurance, years in business), quote CTA, mobile call path.

**Cleaners / landscapers:**
Service list completeness, estimate CTA, service area, before/after photos, recurring service positioning.

**Gyms / fitness / yoga:**
Membership or trial CTA, class schedule visibility, photos, hours, amenities.

**Dentists / chiropractors / healthcare:**
New patient booking path, services list, hours, insurance info, trust signals, review response.

**Barbershops / salons:**
Online booking path, stylist showcase, services and pricing, walk-in vs. appointment clarity.

## Default cold call formula

> "Hey, this is [Name] with Precision Workz here in [City]. I looked at [Business Name]'s Google profile before calling. [Genuine positive observation]. The main thing I noticed is [specific fixable gap]. Would it be okay if I texted you a couple of screenshots showing what I mean?"

After yes to screenshots: "Great — what's the best number?" Close immediately. Do not elaborate.
After no: "Totally fair, I appreciate your time." Clean exit. No second pitch.

## Output format

Generate a complete initial script draft using this structure:

### Cold Call — Primary Opener (20-35 seconds)

Write the opener. It must:
- Reference a specific observation, not a generic claim
- Include a genuine compliment if one is visible
- Name one specific fixable gap
- End with a low-friction screenshot ask

### If They Stay On

The next 30 seconds if they seem interested. Bridge from the specific observation to the audit offer.

### If Receptionist Answers

A version for gatekeepers. Keep it short and specific. Do not pitch to the gatekeeper.

### If They Ask "What Do You Do?"

Plain language. No SEO jargon.

### If They Ask "Are You Selling SEO?"

Use the standard response: "Not exactly. I look at the Google profile, booking path, photos, services, and website flow, then clean up the parts that make it harder for someone to choose and book."

### If They Say Yes to Screenshots

Close immediately: "Great — what's the best number?" Then plan the follow-up sequence.

### If They Say No

Clean exit. No pivot to a second ask.

### Voicemail

Under 20 seconds. Name, what you found, offer to text screenshots, phone number twice.

### Text Follow-Up — After Voicemail

Short. Reference the specific observation. Offer screenshots. Keep it under 3 sentences.

### Cold Email

Under 150 words. Short subject line. Specific observations in bullet points. Screenshot or audit offer. Opt-out line at the end.

### 3-Day Follow-Up Email

Brief. Reference the first contact. One more mention of the specific gap. Easy yes or no question.

### 7-Day Breakup Email

Final note. No pressure. Leave the door open. Opt-out line.
