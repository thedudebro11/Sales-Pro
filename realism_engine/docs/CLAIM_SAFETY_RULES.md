# Claim Safety Rules

## Green claims

Green claims are safe because the user can prove or perform them.

Examples:

- “I noticed your mobile call button is hard to find.”
- “I can show you a few fixable issues.”
- “I can compare your website against nearby competitors.”
- “I can give you a prioritized fix list.”

## Yellow claims

Yellow claims may be true but need softening.

Examples:

Risky:
> “This is costing you customers.”

Safer:
> “This may be making people less likely to call.”

Risky:
> “Your competitors are getting your calls.”

Safer:
> “When people compare options online, these details can affect who they choose.”

## Red claims

Red claims should be removed.

Examples:

- “I can get you top 3.”
- “I guarantee more calls.”
- “The top three get 70% of clicks.”
- “I helped businesses go from invisible to top 3” when no case study exists.
- “Google is punishing your profile.”

## Google-specific rule

Google ranking language should focus on controllable factors, not guaranteed outcomes.

Use:

- “more competitive”
- “visibility factors”
- “profile completeness”
- “comparison against businesses showing above you”
- “may be holding you back”

Avoid:

- “rank top 3”
- “dominate maps”
- “Google will show you first”
- “guaranteed local SEO”
